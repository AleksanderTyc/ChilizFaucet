Chiliz Spicy Faucet - FAQ

Frequently asked questions about the Spicy testnet faucet

General Questions

Q: What is the Spicy testnet?
A: Spicy is a Chiliz test network used by developers for testing smart contracts and dApps before deploying to mainnet. It uses test CHZ that has no real-world value.
Q: Who can use this faucet?
A: This faucet is available to all developers needing test CHZ for building and testing on the Spicy network. You'll need a valid Chiliz wallet address.

Faucet Usage

Q: How much test CHZ can I request?
A: Currently, the faucet distributes 0.1 CHZ per request. There may be daily limits to prevent abuse.
Q: How often can I request funds?
A: You may request funds once every 24 hours. This limit helps ensure fair distribution to all developers.
Q: Why hasn't my transaction arrived?
A: Transactions are usually processed within 1-2 minutes. If delayed, check:
  • Your wallet address was entered correctly
  • Your wallet is connected to the Spicy network
  • The transaction on a Spicy block explorer

Troubleshooting

Q: I received an error message. What should I do?
A: Common solutions:
  • "Funds already claimed": Wait until your next eligible request time
  • "Invalid address": Verify your wallet address format
  • "Rate limited": You've exceeded request limits
Q: The faucet says I'm not eligible. Why?
A: This could be due to:
  • Reaching your daily request limit
  • Suspicious activity from your IP address
  • Temporary service restrictions
Important Note

The faucet is provided as a free service for developers. Please use responsibly to ensure availability for all users.

Advanced

Q: Where can I get more test CHZ?
A: Alternative options include:
  • Community-run faucets
  • Test CHZ offered on developer fora
  • Running a Sepolia validator node
